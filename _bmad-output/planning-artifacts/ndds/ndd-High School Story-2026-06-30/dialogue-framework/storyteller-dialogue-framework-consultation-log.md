## Short Validation

The proposed Step 6 direction is strong and tonally aligned with the GDD/NDD: **lightly cinematic grounded dialogue** fits a nostalgic school social sim that wants recognizable high-school media energy without losing everyday intimacy. It supports warm routine play, sharp social texture, and occasional authored emotional lift.

The MVP voice notes are distinct enough for planning. They define speech behavior through pressure patterns rather than catchphrases: Charlotte controls the room, Oliver deflects pain, Nell protects privacy, Amelia organizes uncertainty, and Leo jokes around fear and accountability. This is the right level for NDD guidance.

The proposed key conversation list covers the main emotional and systemic jobs: arrival solitude, social calibration, five classmate intro/proof beats, club pressure, public seasonal belonging, and neutral semester reflection. Nell being heavier is acceptable because she is explicitly the MVP relationship progression proof case, provided the other four classmates get strong intro scenes plus light recurring ambient presence.

## Recommendations

1. Keep **lightly cinematic grounded dialogue** as the default. It should mean clean, playable, characterful exchanges with selective heightened lines, not long monologues or constant screenplay intensity.

2. Add a small **conversation type taxonomy** to Step 6 so every dialogue asset has a scope label:
   - `Authored Beat Dialogue`: required story scenes and relationship milestones.
   - `Routine Micro-Dialogue`: short repeatable school, lunch, dorm, club, and hallway interactions.
   - `Phone / Message Dialogue`: texts, parent messages, event reminders, group chat fragments, and social media texture.
   - `Reflection Dialogue`: protagonist inner tone, day-end summaries, semester memory ledger.
   - `Variant Lines`: compact condition-based differences for bond, stress, club membership, event attendance, and prior flags.

3. Add missing MVP conversation types without expanding the authored scene count:
   - **Dorm wind-down/check-in micro-moments:** not scenes, but short reflective or phone-based beats after major days.
   - **Phone/social media texture:** event foreshadowing, photo tags, rumor drift, missed invitation traces, class group chatter.
   - **Recovery conversations:** low-energy, high-stress, or missed-event responses that make wellbeing narratively visible.
   - **Transition chatter:** before/after class, lunch line, hallway, club setup/cleanup, walking back to dorm.
   - **Boundary/respect follow-ups:** especially for Nell, but also useful for Oliver care, Amelia help, Leo accountability, and Charlotte social placement.

4. Guard against VN-scope creep with explicit caps:
   - MVP authored story beats remain the existing beat map.
   - Most non-beat dialogue should be 1-4 exchanges, often with only 1 player choice.
   - Full emotional scenes should be rare and attached to named beats.
   - Romance stays as tone/choice/variant inside existing scenes, not separate routes.
   - Every optional dialogue should justify itself by teaching a system, revealing a character pressure, reflecting a player pattern, or foreshadowing a calendar event.

5. Make player responses short full sentences, as proposed, but keep them identity-safe. The protagonist can sound present and human without being overdefined. Avoid options that imply specific past trauma, family conflict, sexual history, fixed social class, or strong personality traits unless selected by the player or established through prior play.

6. Distinguish voices through **syntax, attention, and avoidance**, not verbal gimmicks:
   - Charlotte: precise social framing, polished compliments, soft imperatives, image language, one over-correction under stress.
   - Oliver: warmth first, pain second, joke as redirect, practical care for others before self-disclosure.
   - Nell: compressed observations, privacy tests, dry wit, refusal to narrate herself on demand.
   - Amelia: plans, contingencies, exact terms, helpfulness that arrives too early, fracture when no plan exists.
   - Leo: bluntness, teasing, anti-formal shortcuts, physical/practical details, jokes that sometimes fail.

## Risks

- **Nell gravity risk:** Three Nell beats plus Charlotte/Nell history can make the MVP feel secretly about Nell unless other classmates remain visible through routine dialogue, class moments, phone posts, and Harvest Evening variants.
- **Cinematic inflation risk:** "Lightly cinematic" could become too polished or speech-like. Ground it with interruptions, unfinished thoughts, environmental business, and short player responses.
- **Humor flattening risk:** Sarcasm and meme energy can date quickly or make all students sound alike. Use it as social behavior tied to specific characters and moments, not as universal flavor.
- **Profanity drift risk:** The current restraint is correct. Profanity should mark stress, cruelty, fear, or social boundary crossing, not fill neutral chatter.
- **Phone overuse risk:** Smartphone content should support discovery and social texture, not replace in-person relationship building.
- **Variant explosion risk:** Relationship, club, stress, mood, event, romance, and reputation variants can multiply fast. Prefer small line swaps and gating notes over alternate full scenes.

## Recommended NDD Insert Candidate

### Dialogue Systems Direction

`High School Story` uses **lightly cinematic grounded dialogue**: concise, readable exchanges rooted in everyday school life, with selective heightened lines when a scene earns emotional weight. Student dialogue should feel casual, fast, socially aware, and character-specific. Formal or controlled language appears around teachers, staff, school procedures, public image management, and characters who are actively maintaining a mask.

Most dialogue should be short: quick exchanges, hallway comments, lunch-line remarks, texts, social media fragments, dorm wind-down thoughts, and compact player choices. Longer emotional moments are reserved for authored story beats, relationship milestones, major events, and semester reflection. The game should not read as a visual novel chain of long scenes; authored dialogue sits above the daily loop and is surrounded by routine micro-dialogue.

Humor is allowed and desirable when character-appropriate: sharp irony, wit, sarcasm, awkward humor, and social-media/meme energy can make the school feel alive. Humor should reveal character pressure or social context rather than becoming universal flavor. Profanity may appear realistically but must remain restrained; vulgarity carries emotional or social weight and is not used as punctuation.

Player responses should usually be short full sentences. They should let the protagonist feel present without overdefining backstory, personality, trauma, family history, romantic past, or fixed social status. Response options should express immediate stance, attention, restraint, curiosity, care, avoidance, humor, or boundary-setting.

MVP dialogue should use five asset types:

| Dialogue Type | Function | MVP Scope Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Authored Beat Dialogue | Required story beats, classmate introductions, Nell progression, major events, and semester reflection. | Full scenes only for named MVP beats. |
| Routine Micro-Dialogue | Hallway, class, lunch, dorm, club setup/cleanup, travel, recovery, and short check-ins. | Usually 1-4 exchanges; often one or no player choice. |
| Phone / Message Dialogue | Parent contact, event foreshadowing, social media, group chatter, reminders, and rumor texture. | Supports discovery and atmosphere without replacing in-person relationship building. |
| Reflection Dialogue | Subtle protagonist inner tone and memory ledger interpretation. | Framing only; preserves player authorship. |
| Variant Lines | Bond, stress, mood, club membership, event attendance, romance hint, missed chance, and prior-flag differences. | Prefer compact line swaps over alternate full scenes. |

MVP character voices:

- **Charlotte:** polished, socially fluent, precise; compliments can double as control; when shaken, she over-corrects.
- **Oliver:** warm, easy, deflective; jokes to keep concern away; rarely says pain directly.
- **Nell:** dry, guarded, observant; says less than she notices; uses sharpness to protect privacy.
- **Amelia:** composed, practical, exact; speaks in plans and contingencies; cracks appear when control fails.
- **Leo:** blunt, teasing, anti-formal; uses humor as shield; surprisingly attentive in concrete details.

MVP should protect scope by keeping romance, deeper friendship, and full vulnerability scenes as future-facing layers unless they fit inside existing named beats. Nell receives the only MVP partial relationship arc as proof of the relationship model; other classmates remain future-arc candidates through strong intro beats, routine presence, and event variants.
