# Storyteller Classmate Arc Consultation Log

This is the running Storyteller artifact for future-facing classmate arc consultation. Handoff files remain input sources; new classmate arc reviews should be appended here.

## Consultation 1: Eleanor "Nell" Graves

### Overall Verdict

Nell's skeleton is narratively strong, emotionally mature, and well aligned with the game's core emotional priority: nostalgia into attachment into bittersweet reflection. Its central strength is that it treats school drama not as a simple secret plot, but as a question of who gets to tell whose story.

The main risk is gravitational when Nell is coupled too tightly with Charlotte. On her own, Nell's arc is intimate and relationship-shaped. If the old rumor becomes too large or too public, though, she could start to feel like the emotional center of the school.

### Arc Strength and Creativity

Nell's arc is especially elegant because the relationship test is not "unlock the secret" but "learn not to treat closeness as entitlement." The Stranger, Acquaintance, Friend, Close Friend, and Final Path phases all work:

- Stranger: the player meets the rumor before the person.
- Acquaintance: the player learns boundaries around authorship and privacy.
- Friend: Nell allows partial truth without full exposure.
- Close Friend: she tests chosen visibility.
- Final Path: closeness is confirmed without ownership.

The strongest beats are 2, 3, 7, and 10 because they keep the arc rooted in authorship, consent, and controlled visibility. This avoids the "fix the goth girl" trap and makes Nell's full arc a strong relationship model.

Primary risk: beat 8 could become too large if the old rumor resurfaces as a major public event. That would increase Nell's main-story gravity. It should remain local or socially specific unless the full game later deliberately expands it.

### Recommended Micro-Edits

1. For beat 8, specify a local/social resurfacing or reframing of the rumor rather than a schoolwide dramatic event.
2. For beat 5, add that understanding Nell's grief does not equal resolving Charlotte's accountability.

## Consultation 2: Charlotte "Lottie" Fairchild

### Overall Verdict

Charlotte's skeleton is narratively strong because it frames social power as both gift and armor. It avoids reducing her to "popular girl learns to be nice" by preserving her taste, polish, charm, and social fluency as real strengths.

The main risk is over-symmetry with Nell. Charlotte's accountability arc is strongest when it reveals her own relationship to status and denial, not when it simply mirrors Eleanor's wound as a karmic lesson.

### Arc Strength and Creativity

Charlotte's arc works because it does not punish her for beauty, polish, fashion, or social intelligence. Her gifts are real; the wound is that she has learned to use those gifts as armor and hierarchy.

The progression is sound:

- Stranger: charm and hierarchy appear together.
- Acquaintance: the player sees private warmth or over-correction.
- Friend: "Lottie" appears as a less managed Charlotte, not a separate secret self.
- Close Friend: the family/status machinery becomes visible.
- Final Path: accountability must cost her something in public.

The strongest beats are 1, 3, 8, and 9. Beat 3 is particularly useful because it shows Charlotte can be kind when kindness does not yet threaten her status.

Primary risk: beat 8's public image crisis may mirror Eleanor too neatly. It should reveal the same social mechanism rather than create a simple karmic reversal.

### Recommended Micro-Edits

1. For beat 8, frame the crisis as echoing the mechanism of Eleanor's isolation rather than repeating it one-for-one.
2. For beat 10, make accountability practical: she changes how she uses social power in rooms where no one rewards her for it.

### Cross-Arc Note: Nell and Charlotte

Together, the arcs form a compelling thematic pair:

- Nell protects authorship because truth has been weaponized against her.
- Charlotte protects image because truth threatens belonging.
- Both are about control of narrative, seen from opposite sides of the table.

This is excellent, but potent. Their shared history should remain one strong social wound among several, not the secret that explains the whole school.

## Consultation 3: Oliver "Ollie" Grant

### Overall Verdict

Oliver's skeleton is strong, usable, and emotionally broader than the flat "injured athlete learns to rest" version of the trope. Its best move is that the injury is not merely a medical problem. His body has become a public container for family hope, school pride, team culture, and future opportunity. That gives the arc enough breadth for a full 10-beat future-facing skeleton.

The arc question is clean: can Oliver learn that value is not the same thing as being unbreakable? It is simple enough to guide scenes, but not simplistic, because the proposed beats keep sport, charm, social warmth, ambition, and pressure alive at the same time.

### Arc Strength and Creativity

The strongest creative choice is that Oliver's charm remains real. He is not secretly bitter under a fake golden-boy mask; he is warm, liked, generous, and overloaded. That makes the player relationship more humane. The player is not discovering that the public Oliver is false. They are discovering that the public Oliver is incomplete.

Beats 2 and 3 create a good intimacy ramp: the player notices what others miss, then learns that help itself feels threatening to Oliver. Beat 4 is especially strong because it externalizes the wound. A younger teammate copying "walk it off" behavior turns Oliver's private self-damage into visible culture, which is more interesting than another private pain confession.

Beat 5 gives the arc its necessary width: sport, family hope, and future opportunity collide. This is the beat that keeps the skeleton from becoming just a rest-and-recovery lesson.

### Phase Progression

The relationship phases make sense:

- Stranger: the player sees admired grit and the reward structure around self-damage.
- Acquaintance: the player notices contradiction and is gently held at a distance through humor.
- Friend: Oliver's choices begin to affect others, and the player sees why stopping is costly.
- Close Friend: fear becomes speakable, and choices around disclosure/recovery become real.
- Final Path: care is reframed as respect, not pity or ownership.

The only phase pressure point is beat 8. It currently carries a lot: performance, honest recovery, partial truth, and changed perception. That is good future-facing material, but it may need slightly sharper language so it reads as an arc hinge rather than a menu of possible outcomes.

### Cliche Risks

The skeleton already avoids the largest cliche by refusing to make sport bad. The wound is not athletic ambition; the wound is the social conversion of Oliver's body into a public resource.

Remaining risks:

1. "Learn to rest" simplicity. Beat 10 must not imply simple withdrawal from sport.
2. Pity romance. The romantic route must avoid protective control or the player becoming Oliver's private nurse.
3. Noble injury martyrdom. Beat 6 should show the cost of damaging reliability without making pain look beautiful or heroic.
4. Too-clean disclosure. Beat 8 should allow partial truth, boundaries, and changed participation rather than a single cathartic confession.

### Enough Material Beyond MVP

Yes. The full arc has enough material beyond "Walk It Off" because it expands across four pressure systems:

- Body: injury, pain masking, recovery habits, limits.
- School: applause, team culture, social media, public pride.
- Family/future: opportunity, obligation, fear of disappointing others.
- Relationship: learning to receive care without feeling reduced by it.

That is sufficient for a 10-beat future arc, especially if later beats preserve ambiguity around sport rather than pushing him toward a single correct life choice.

### Recommended Micro-Edits

1. In beat 4, clarify that Oliver's recognition is uncomfortable because he is both harmed by the culture and a carrier of it. This gives him responsibility without blame.
2. In beat 5, name at least one non-sport pressure vector in the function line, such as family finances, scholarship possibility, parental pride, or community expectation. "Future opportunity" is good, but one concrete pressure would make the beat sturdier.
3. In beat 8, tighten the hinge: frame it as Oliver choosing what truth his body is allowed to tell, rather than a broad choice between performance/recovery/partial truth.
4. In beat 10, preserve sport as a possible love rather than a defeated identity: strength becomes changing his relationship to performance, not simply leaving performance behind.

### Suggested Wording Adjustments

- Beat 4: "A younger student or teammate copies Oliver's 'walk it off' behavior, forcing him to see that the culture hurting him is also being modeled through him."
- Beat 5: "Sport, family hope, and future opportunity collide; the player learns the concrete stakes behind stopping, such as scholarship pressure, parental pride, or fear of becoming a burden."
- Beat 8: "The player helps Oliver decide what truth his body is allowed to tell: continued performance, honest recovery, or a bounded disclosure that changes how others see him."
- Beat 10: "Oliver defines strength as knowing when not to break himself, ending the arc with agency and a healthier relationship to sport rather than simple withdrawal from it."

## Consultation 4: Amelia Price

### Overall Verdict

Amelia's skeleton is strong, emotionally legible, and distinct from the existing classmates. It avoids the thin "stressed top student learns to relax" version of the archetype by rooting excellence in survival, parentification, and the need for one reliable system in a life where other systems have failed her quietly.

The arc has enough ache for the game's nostalgic adult audience because it touches a very recognizable school memory: the student who always had the answer may not have been thriving. She may have been holding the room together because no one was holding her.

Main-story gravity risk is low-to-moderate. Amelia has strong thematic ties to Leo and to school systems, but her conflict does not currently threaten to become the hidden central plot. The main watchpoint is the Science Showcase / academic opportunity track: if it becomes the primary institutional storyline for the whole semester, Amelia could drift into "true protagonist of school pressure." Keep her arc intimate, procedural, and relational rather than making it the master plot of academic life.

### Arc Strength and Creativity

The best creative choice is that Amelia's competence is preserved as real. The story does not need to humble her, expose her as fake, or teach her that ambition is bad. Her excellence is a tool, a shelter, and a map out. That gives the arc dignity.

The core wound is especially playable: Amelia believes chaos will catch her if she stops being useful, excellent, and controlled. That can express itself through systems the game already has: scheduling, study sessions, homework, stress, teacher approval, Science Club, and recommendation pressure. Her behavioral tell, the emergency folder or pouch, is excellent because it makes invisible labor visible without a speech.

Beat 5 is one of the most interesting beats because it gives Amelia a morally complicated feeling. Leo or another less controlled student receiving sudden adult attention creates envy, resentment, and fear without making Amelia petty. It exposes a painful question: what if years of consistency are less visible than one dramatic moment of potential?

Beat 9 is also important because Amelia's aroace Best Friend path needs its own emotional vocabulary. "Shared routines, practical care, and permission to be unready" is exactly the right territory. It frames intimacy as chosen reliability, not as romance with the romance removed.

### Phase Progression

The relationship progression works:

- Stranger: the player sees benchmark competence and faint loneliness.
- Acquaintance: the player notices that readiness is load-bearing.
- Friend: help becomes the test, and school pressure begins to intersect with home pressure.
- Close Friend: Amelia can name the terror underneath usefulness.
- Final Path: deep non-romantic intimacy lets her be cared for without earning it.

Beat 4 is the correct turn into friendship because accepting help is more intimate for Amelia than giving it. Beat 6 then deepens the arc by letting home pressure interrupt school order in a concrete way. Beat 7 arrives at the right time: after the player has seen enough evidence that "I don't know who I am when I am not useful" feels earned rather than announced.

The only phase pressure is between beats 7 and 8. They are both strong, but close in emotional territory: identity confession and unmasked need. Beat 8 should be sharpened as an external choice or deadline, not another inward admission.

### Cliche Risks

The skeleton already avoids several obvious traps: it does not punish ambition, does not make competence fake, does not make Leo automatically right, and does not treat aroace intimacy as lesser.

Remaining risks:

1. "Stressed top student learns to relax." Beat 10 must not resolve into rest, looseness, or spontaneity as the moral answer. Amelia should gain selfhood, not simply become less organized.
2. Parentification exposition dump. Beat 6 should stay behavioral: interruptions, errands, messages, missed meals, forms, sibling logistics, or money/time friction.
3. Teacher's pet flattening. Early beats should show that teacher trust is both reward and burden, not just privilege.
4. Leo-as-truth-teller imbalance. Beat 5 must preserve Amelia's rational trust in process; Leo's freer stance should challenge her, not defeat her.
5. Aroace path underweighting. Beat 9 needs to feel as emotionally climactic as a romantic route would for another classmate.

### Enough Material Beyond MVP

Yes. The full arc has more than enough material beyond "The Perfect Answer" because it can unfold across five pressure systems:

- Academic identity: grades, teacher trust, recommendations, advanced programs.
- Invisible labor: forms, reminders, supplies, planning, emergency preparedness.
- Home instability: parentification, logistical demands, disrupted study time.
- Peer comparison: Leo's different relationship to rules, attention, and urgency.
- Intimacy without romance: practical care, routine, being unready, being wanted rather than needed.

This is a particularly good full-game skeleton because it supports small, recurring interactions. Amelia does not need one explosive secret. Her arc can be built from tiny failures of containment: a forgotten item, an interrupted plan, a too-fast apology, a homemade recipe carried but never made.

### Recommended Micro-Edits

1. In beat 3, clarify that the academic opportunity is not just achievement bait but a perceived route to safety or escape. This keeps ambition emotionally grounded.
2. In beat 5, soften the comparison with Leo so Amelia's reaction is painful and human, not resentful in a simplistic way. The issue is not that Leo gets attention; it is that inconsistency suddenly becomes visible in a way consistency rarely does.
3. In beat 8, make the choice external and specific, such as a showcase deadline, recommendation packet, group project, or crisis where flawless output would require hiding real need.
4. In beat 10, preserve competence and ambition while shifting ownership: Amelia still plans, studies, and achieves, but the future is chosen for desire as well as escape.

### Suggested Wording Adjustments

- Beat 3: "A recommendation, advanced program, teacher approval moment, or academic opportunity raises the stakes of her 'perfect' performance because success feels like proof, safety, and a possible way out."
- Beat 5: "Leo or another less controlled student receives unexpected adult attention, making Amelia confront how one visible flash of need or potential can be valued more loudly than years of quiet consistency."
- Beat 8: "A deadline or responsibility forces Amelia to choose between flawless output and honest need: preserving the mask or letting someone see the unfinished, unsupported version of her before everything is handled."
- Beat 9: "Best Friend route confirms deep non-romantic intimacy through trust, shared routines, practical care, and the rare permission to be unready, unhelpful, and still wanted."
- Beat 10: "Amelia pursues a future that belongs to her: still competent, still ambitious, but no longer built only from grades, recommendations, escape planning, and being indispensable."

## Consultation 5: Leo Ward

### Overall Verdict

Leo's skeleton is strong, emotionally charged, and distinct from the rest of the cast. It avoids the thin "rebel skater with a heart of gold" version of the archetype by making his protectiveness morally useful and morally dangerous at the same time.

The arc question is excellent because it turns the expected rebellion story inward: Leo is not simply resisting authority. He is resisting hope. "Can Leo risk wanting a future before he knows whether he can reach it?" gives the full arc a deeper engine than discipline trouble, skating, or school conflict alone.

Main-story gravity risk is moderate. Leo naturally touches system failure, bullying, public humiliation, discipline, adult neglect, and Amelia's counterpoint worldview. Those are big social themes. The arc stays balanced if Leo's story remains one student's pattern of intervention and avoidance, not the game's master thesis on whether school systems work.

### Arc Strength and Creativity

The strongest creative choice is that Leo's care is not sanitized. He can genuinely protect someone and still make the situation worse. That preserves moral discomfort, which is exactly what "The Phone He Broke" needs. His motive can be legible without becoming a pardon.

The second strong choice is separating punishment from vulnerability. Leo can survive detention, labels, and teacher suspicion because those confirm a story he already knows. Trying, wanting, and failing are more frightening because they would expose a self he cannot dismiss with a joke. That gives beats 6, 7, and 10 real emotional lift.

His behavioral tell is also excellent: pretending not to listen, then acting on everything he heard. It makes loyalty visible without turning him into a speech machine. Food tossed without comment, a repaired object, or a route quietly adjusted around someone's fear can all carry more feeling than a confession.

### Phase Progression

The relationship progression works:

- Stranger: the player sees protection, reputation, and moral mess in one incident.
- Acquaintance: the player notices competence and care hidden behind carelessness.
- Friend: Leo's intervention style helps and harms, and the player sees why he distrusts slow procedure.
- Close Friend: he admits that effort and future desire scare him more than punishment.
- Final Path: intimacy requires honesty without comedy, spectacle, or protective anger as armor.

Beat 4 is the right friendship hinge because it refuses to let the player flatten Leo into either problem or hero. Beat 5 usefully places Amelia beside him as a mirror, but it should not steal the center of his arc. Beat 8 is crucial because it converts moral insight into action: Leo must repair harm caused by his method, not merely explain his motive.

The only phase pressure point is beat 6. "Offered a path forward" is right, but it needs one or two concrete lanes so it does not feel generic. A repair task, town-edge apprenticeship, practical project, or adult who notices his skill would make the future feel tangible rather than abstract.

### Cliche Risks

The skeleton already avoids several major traps: chaos is not treated as automatically authentic, Amelia is not simply wrong, and Leo's protective motive does not erase harm.

Remaining risks:

1. "Heart of gold" simplification. Leo's kindness should not be revealed as the whole truth. His avoidance, collateral damage, and fear of effort must remain real.
2. Cool-rulebreaker glamour. Detention, broken property, and public confrontation should carry social and emotional consequences, not only style.
3. Protector romance possessiveness. If romantic, his loyalty must become honesty and restraint, not surveillance, jealousy, or "I fight for you" fantasy.
4. Amelia-as-foil overuse. Their conflict is rich, but Leo needs independent stakes outside the Amelia mirror.
5. Comedy as permanent escape. Jokes should sometimes fail, wound, or expose him rather than always smoothing the room.

### Enough Material Beyond MVP

Yes. The full arc has enough material beyond "The Phone He Broke" because it can unfold across five pressure systems:

- Justice: intervention, humiliation, retaliation, privacy, and repair.
- Reputation: teachers see incidents, some students see motives, Leo performs carelessness.
- Future: skills, projects, recommendations, adult attention, fear of wanting more.
- Friendship: humor, low-polish loyalty, spontaneous plans, honest accountability.
- Amelia mirror: slow procedure versus immediate care, with both positions carrying truth.

This gives Leo plenty of future-facing canon without needing a huge scandal. His arc can stay grounded in repeated moments where he has to decide whether to act, how to act, and whether to stay afterward to clean up the damage.

### Recommended Micro-Edits

1. In beat 3, specify that Leo avoids a chance because wanting it would make failure personal. This brings the core wound forward earlier.
2. In beat 4, name collateral damage more explicitly so the beat cannot be read as "Leo was right, adults are slow."
3. In beat 6, make the path forward concrete: a repair project, community task, apprenticeship-like adult attention, creative work, or a recommendation tied to demonstrated skill.
4. In beat 8, include the protected student's privacy as a possible repair target. This is important because Leo's intervention can accidentally increase attention on the person he meant to protect.
5. In beat 10, preserve his humor and intolerance for cruelty while adding effort and accountability. He should not become polished; he should become answerable.

### Suggested Wording Adjustments

- Beat 3: "Leo avoids a chance that would prove he wants more, using jokes, lateness, rule-breaking, or 'not my thing' energy because failing at something wanted would hurt more than being dismissed as careless."
- Beat 4: "His protective anger helps someone but creates fallout, forcing the player to see both the value of immediate intervention and the collateral damage Leo must learn to own."
- Beat 6: "Leo is offered a concrete path forward, such as a repair project, practical recommendation, creative task, or adult attention tied to a skill he cannot joke away, and almost ruins it first."
- Beat 8: "He must repair harm caused by his method, not only defend his motive: apology, practical restitution, protecting the victim's privacy, or accepting consequences without turning it into a joke."
- Beat 10: "Leo risks wanting a future before knowing whether he can reach it, ending the arc with humor, directness, effort, and accountability rather than simple rebellion."
