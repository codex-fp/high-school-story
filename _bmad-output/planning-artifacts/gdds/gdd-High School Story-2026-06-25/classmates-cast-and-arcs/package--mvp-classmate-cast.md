## Scope

- Eleanor "Nell" Graves receives the MVP partial 3-beat arc.
- The other 4 classmates receive 1 introduction beat each.
- Total MVP relationship/story beats: 7.
- Full romance is not required in MVP. Chemistry, early signals, and future potential are enough.

Relationship access rule:

- Core classmate story beats must not require membership in a specific club.
- Club membership can add variants, insider context, better preparation, lower Stress, stronger outcomes, extra dialogue, club identity texture, or additional flags.
- Route variants are alternate access paths to one canonical relationship beat. The first valid variant encountered advances the beat state and suppresses duplicate variants.

## Charlotte "Lottie" Fairchild

**Working name:** Charlotte "Lottie" Fairchild.

**Core archetype:** Hyper-polished Queen Bee / fashionable perfect daughter.

**How the archetype is made specific/subverted:**  
Charlotte is not a simple vain mean girl. Her family is new money trying to look old money, and Charlotte has been shaped into proof that the family belongs. Her polish is trained, defensive, and guilt-reinforced. Eleanor knew her before the crown hardened, which makes Charlotte's current perfection feel fragile.

**Short personality summary:**  
Immaculate, controlled, charming, socially fluent, and hard to catch unprepared. She can make social hierarchy feel like warmth until the edge appears.

**Social role in class/school:**  
Visible social center and taste-maker. She organizes attention: who gets included, photographed, complimented, or politely erased. She and Oliver are treated as public symbols of school success.

**Academic tendency / subject affinity:**  
Good overall, especially in subjects involving presentation, language, and social confidence. Her grades are part of the image, but she is not the cast's true academic obsessive.

**Club/event ties:**  
Strong Class Integration Day tie. Strong Harvest Evening social/FOMO potential. Light Oliver tie through school-spirit photos and public-image assumptions. No core club tie required.

**Preferred locations / schedule habits:**  
Courtyard, hallway/common area, cafeteria center tables, school entrance before public events, cafe when seen with the right people, Harvest Evening social stalls.

**Preferences / favorite hooks:**  
Social Preference: large group. Free Time Preference: social activity. Music Preference: pop. Media Preference: romance/drama. Food Preference: coffee/tea.

Flavor hooks: fashion, carefully curated pop playlists, stylish coffee orders, glossy teen dramas, elegant accessories, group social energy with private escape moments.

**Best repeatable interaction types:**  
Talk, lunch together, cafe hangout, small stylish gift, event invite, Social Media interaction, check-in after public pressure.

**Possible romanceability and authored orientation:**  
Accepted: bisexual, romanceable for compatible protagonists. MVP can show social chemistry, teasing, or private softness, but full romance should wait for later content.

**What the player first notices:**  
Charlotte looks like school is a stage and she already knows every mark, entrance, and audience reaction.

**What the player might misunderstand:**  
That she is naturally elite and effortlessly cruel. In truth, much of her polish is a survival system, and her coldness toward Eleanor is tied to guilt.

**Intro beat premise:**  
**"The Photo Everyone Wants To Be In"** - During Class Integration Day, a class photo, school profile board, or integration display needs to be arranged. Charlotte naturally takes control because she "has an eye" for presentation. On the surface she is helpful, elegant, and efficient: adjusting outfits, placing people, smoothing the room. Underneath, every placement says who belongs near the center and who is allowed to become background.

Charlotte gives compliments that sound polished but carry status pressure, such as calling someone's jacket "brave" or gently implying that the protagonist would look better standing somewhere else. Eleanor may pass by or stand aside while a classmate jokes about whether she should be in the photo, for example, "Wouldn't she curse the camera?" Charlotte does not need to openly attack Eleanor; a controlled silence, a formal "Eleanor can stand wherever she likes," or a neat redirection is enough to erase her.

The player can accept Charlotte's social frame, notice who is being excluded, suggest Eleanor or another marginal student be included, or respond to Charlotte's sharp compliment with humor, silence, or a counter. The beat establishes Charlotte's power over image, taste, social placement, and deniable cruelty while planting the Eleanor tension without resolving it.

**Partial 3-beat arc candidate:**  
Strong candidate, but not selected. Charlotte's full arc is powerful, but MVP should keep her as a charged supporting presence while Eleanor carries the trust-focused partial arc.

## Oliver "Ollie" Grant

**Working name:** Oliver "Ollie" Grant.

**Core archetype:** Jock / golden-boy athlete.

**How the archetype is made specific/subverted:**  
Oliver is not just the popular athlete. He is hiding an injury or chronic pain because sport is tied to family hope, future opportunity, and self-worth. The school praised him for playing hurt, teaching him that self-damage looks like courage.

**Short personality summary:**  
Warm, easygoing, physically confident, protective of the team's image, and good at making pain look like nothing. His charm is real, but it is also camouflage.

**Social role in class/school:**  
Golden athlete and public symbol. He is admired by peers, useful to school pride, and often paired socially with Charlotte as an image of success.

**Academic tendency / subject affinity:**  
Average-to-good if supported, strongest in PE/physical activity and practical teamwork. May struggle when injury, fatigue, or Stress interfere.

**Club/event ties:**  
No core club tie. Strong Harvest Evening casual social potential. Light Charlotte tie through school photos/social assumptions. Sports/team culture sits mostly adjacent to the core club system.

**Preferred locations / schedule habits:**  
Outdoor campus area, gym/field implied spaces, hallway near team friends, convenience store for quick drinks/snacks, park or quiet edge of campus when hiding pain.

**Preferences / favorite hooks:**  
Social Preference: small group. Free Time Preference: outdoor activity. Music Preference: hip-hop/R&B. Media Preference: sports/action. Food Preference: pizza/fries.

Flavor hooks: energetic playlists, convenience-store drinks, casual group hangouts, physical activities, practical encouragement rather than pity.

**Best repeatable interaction types:**  
Talk, quick check-in, snack/gift, low-pressure hangout, recovery support, event invite, gentle observation after practice/training.

**Possible romanceability and authored orientation:**  
Accepted: straight, romanceable for compatible protagonists. MVP can show warmth or public-image chemistry but does not need romance commitment.

**What the player first notices:**  
Oliver looks effortless: athletic, popular, approachable, and already surrounded by people.

**What the player might misunderstand:**  
That he is carefree and uncomplicated. In truth, his body has become the place everyone stores their hopes.

**Intro beat premise:**  
**"Walk It Off"** - After a school-spirit sports moment, casual practice, or PE-adjacent scene, a younger teammate tries to keep going despite obvious pain because "Ollie would walk it off." Oliver instinctively says yes to helping, demonstrating, or staying longer than he should, even though the player can catch a hidden wince or too-careful movement.

The surface read is warm and heroic: Oliver is reliable, encouraging, and everyone expects him to be the person who can push through. The darker read is that his self-damage has become a lesson other students are copying. If the player pays attention, they can see him almost tell the younger student to stop, then swallow it because doing so would expose that Oliver himself is not fine.

The player can encourage Oliver's public grit, quietly check on his pain, support the younger teammate, or notice the contradiction without forcing a confrontation. The beat establishes Oliver's charm, the "walk it off" culture around him, and the first hint that being admired is part of what traps him.

**Partial 3-beat arc candidate:**  
Possible, but not selected. His arc is strong, yet it is less connected to the MVP club/event spine than Eleanor's.

## Eleanor "Nell" Graves

**Working name:** Eleanor "Nell" Graves.

**Core archetype:** Goth girl / rumor girl / wounded writer.

**How the archetype is made specific/subverted:**  
Eleanor's goth image is not only aesthetic. It is a defense built after private heartbreak writing was exposed and flattened into an ugly school rumor. She would rather be misunderstood than exposed again.

**Short personality summary:**  
Guarded, sharp, observant, literary, intense, and more tender than she lets the room see. She can be cutting when cornered, but much of the edge is a warning label.

**Social role in class/school:**  
Rumor-shaped outsider. Students think they know her story before they know her. Her presence tests whether the player accepts school narratives or listens for the person under them.

**Academic tendency / subject affinity:**  
Strong in literature, writing, media, and interpretive subjects. May disengage in public discussion when she feels watched or misread.

**Club/event ties:**  
Primary Literary Club tie. Primary Zine Deadline Crisis tie. Strong Social Media/reputation hook. Strong library/quiet-space presence. Charlotte rupture sits behind her public isolation.

**Preferred locations / schedule habits:**  
Library corners, quiet hallway edges, cafeteria margins, Literary Club room, park in cooler evening weather, Social Media under controlled/anonymous conditions.

**Preferences / favorite hooks:**  
Social Preference: quiet company. Free Time Preference: creative activity. Music Preference: indie/alternative. Media Preference: indie/art. Food Preference: coffee/tea.

Flavor hooks: dark romantic writing, poetry, gothic or literary media, tea/black coffee, quiet one-on-one time, walking instead of crowded hangouts, careful messages.

**Best repeatable interaction types:**  
Text, quiet talk, library encounter, share/ask about writing, low-pressure check-in, small thoughtful gift, park walk, Literary Club activity variant.

**Possible romanceability and authored orientation:**  
Accepted: bi/pan, romanceable for compatible protagonists in the full game. MVP should focus on trust and emotional safety; any romantic signal should be subtle and optional.

**What the player first notices:**  
Dark clothes, controlled distance, sharp eyes, and the sense that the room has already judged her before she speaks.

**What the player might misunderstand:**  
That she is cold, hostile, or performatively edgy. In truth, she is protecting the last pieces of privacy she still controls.

**Intro beat premise:**  
**"The Version Everyone Knows"** - The player first encounters Eleanor through the school's rumor lens: a joke, warning, Social Media fragment, or public moment where others expect the protagonist to accept the flattened story about her. The beat gives the player a first chance to withhold judgment.

**Partial 3-beat arc candidate:**  
Selected. Eleanor best proves relationship progression as trust rather than grind.

### Eleanor MVP 3-Beat Partial Arc

#### Beat 1: The Version Everyone Knows

**Relationship phase represented:** Stranger Beat.

**Intended movement:** Stranger toward Acquaintance. The player meets the rumor before meeting the truth.

**Required systemic interaction buildup:**  
Initial contact or 1-2 light interactions can precede it, but this beat can also serve as the first meaningful contact.

**Unlock/context requirements:**  
Early semester. Hallway, cafeteria, library, Social Media clue, or public comment about Eleanor. No Literary Club membership required.

**Premise:**  
The player witnesses a joke, warning, or social media fragment about Eleanor's old writing-profile rumor. Eleanor notices the moment and expects the player to react like everyone else. The player can laugh along, stay silent, deflect, or refuse to flatten her into the rumor.

**Story flag created:**  
`nell_player_withheld_judgment` or `nell_player_joined_rumor`.

**Member/non-member access path:**  
Non-member route: hallway/cafeteria/library/Social Media encounter.  
Literary Club route: player hears a club-side version of why Eleanor is "complicated" around shared writing.

**How it sets up future Friend-phase arc:**  
Shows that Eleanor's obstacle is not merely sadness; it is being narrated by others.

#### Beat 2: A Piece With No Name

**Relationship phase represented:** Acquaintance Beat 1.

**Intended movement:** Surface familiarity toward specific curiosity. Eleanor becomes more than "the goth rumor girl."

**Required systemic interaction buildup:**  
Roughly 2-3 meaningful interactions after Beat 1, such as texting, quiet talk, library encounter, small thoughtful gift, or respectful check-in.

**Unlock/context requirements:**  
Acquaintance threshold or near-threshold Bond, plus a quiet location or message context. Literary Club membership can provide an insider route but is not required.

**Premise:**  
The player encounters an anonymous fragment of writing: a poem, zine draft, margin note, or post that feels too specific to ignore. Eleanor does not fully admit ownership at first. The player can pry, mock, pretend not to notice, or give her control over whether the piece is discussed.

**Story flag created:**  
`nell_player_respected_private_writing` or `nell_player_pushed_for_context`.

**Member/non-member access path:**  
Non-member route: library encounter, text thread, anonymous Social Media clue, or quiet-location meeting.  
Literary Club route: the player recognizes a submission style or sees a draft in the zine workflow.

**How it sets up future Friend-phase arc:**  
Establishes writing as vulnerability and control over authorship as the relationship's emotional test.

#### Beat 3: Zine Deadline Crisis

**Relationship phase represented:** Acquaintance Beat 2 / Doorway Into Friendship.

**Intended movement:** End of acquaintance phase and first real trust flag. This should make friendship feel reachable, not complete the Friend-phase arc.

**Required systemic interaction buildup:**  
Roughly 4-6 meaningful interactions across the acquaintance phase, including at least one sign that the player respected Eleanor's boundaries.

**Unlock/context requirements:**  
Zine Deadline Crisis window, sufficient Bond/context, and either Literary Club involvement, Eleanor's invitation, public zine context, text clue, or guest/helper opportunity.

**Premise:**  
A piece in the zine workflow risks being published, misattributed, recognized, or edited in a way that violates the author's boundaries. The crisis echoes Eleanor's old wound without repeating the old leak cheaply. The player can help protect consent, clarify authorship, support Eleanor privately, or prioritize public smoothness.

**Story flag created:**  
`nell_trusted_player_with_authorship_boundary` or `nell_saw_player_protect_consent`.

**Member/non-member access path:**  
Literary Club member route: player acts from inside the zine process with better context and more preparation.  
Non-member route: Eleanor asks the player for a bounded trust-sensitive task, or the public zine issue creates a visible opportunity to help.  
First valid route wins: whichever route resolves the beat advances the same canonical beat state and suppresses duplicate variants.

**How it sets up future Friend-phase arc:**  
The player has not learned everything and has not repaired Charlotte/Eleanor. Instead, Eleanor has evidence that closeness might not mean losing control of her story.

## Amelia Price

**Working name:** Amelia Price.

**Core archetype:** Teacher's pet / top student / teachers' favorite.

**How the archetype is made specific/subverted:**  
Amelia is not perfect because life is easy. She is parentified and invisibly neglected; school is the only system where effort produces order. University is her imagined exit route.

**Short personality summary:**  
Prepared, precise, composed, quietly exhausted, helpful in practical ways, and easily threatened by chaos. She can sound judgmental when she is actually scared.

**Social role in class/school:**  
Teacher-trusted organizer and academic benchmark. She represents the student who makes the system work because she needs it to work.

**Academic tendency / subject affinity:**  
Very strong across academic subjects, especially structured work requiring Discipline. Science Club is tied to her recommendation strategy.

**Club/event ties:**  
Primary Science Club tie. Primary Science Showcase Sprint tie. Strong Week 20 exam pressure. Mirror conflict with Leo through Recommendation Slot.

**Preferred locations / schedule habits:**  
Library/study room, front of classrooms, Science Club room, teacher-adjacent spaces, dorm study blocks, cafeteria edge when working through lunch.

**Preferences / favorite hooks:**  
Social Preference: planned activities. Free Time Preference: study. Music Preference: classical/soundtrack. Media Preference: documentary. Food Preference: homemade food.

Flavor hooks: structured study playlists, practical gifts, quiet study sessions, purposeful hangouts, packed lunches, reliable routines.

**Best repeatable interaction types:**  
Study together, homework help, Science Club activity, practical check-in, lunch while studying, supportive text around deadlines, small useful gift.

**Possible romanceability and authored orientation:**  
Accepted: aromantic asexual, not romanceable. Full game should support a meaningful Best Friend path where the player matters without romantic framing.

**What the player first notices:**  
Polished homework, calm answers, teacher trust, and the faint sense that she is already three tasks ahead of everyone else.

**What the player might misunderstand:**  
That she is privileged, judgmental, or naturally perfect. In truth, perfection is how she keeps panic organized.

**Intro beat premise:**  
**"The Perfect Answer"** - During a lesson, teacher check, or academic setup scene, Amelia answers a difficult question almost before anyone else has time to think. The room reads it as typical Amelia: perfect work, perfect timing, teacher approval. But the player can notice that she does not enjoy the victory. She checks the teacher's reaction like someone confirming that the ground is still under her feet.

Afterward, the scene reveals the second layer: Amelia has already prepared the relevant form, club signup, deadline note, study plan, or backup copy that everyone else is only now discovering they need. It is impressive and a little funny, but also sad. Amelia is not just organized; she is functioning as her own parent, secretary, and emergency plan.

The player can admire her competence, ask whether she ever stops planning, accept practical help from her, or notice the exhaustion beneath the polish. The beat establishes Amelia's teacher-trusted perfection and the hidden reason for it: if she is not ahead of the chaos, the chaos catches her.

**Partial 3-beat arc candidate:**  
Strong academically, but not selected. Her arc is better as an MVP intro seed tied to Science Club and future pressure.

## Leo Ward

**Working name:** Leo Ward.

**Core archetype:** Rebel skater / rule-breaker outsider.

**How the archetype is made specific/subverted:**  
Leo is not just a lazy troublemaker. He has a strong sensitivity to cruelty and cannot walk past bullying. His problem is that his justice becomes impulsive and can reproduce the same humiliation he hates.

**Short personality summary:**  
Funny, evasive, sharp, protective, restless, allergic to authority, and more observant than he wants adults to know.

**Social role in class/school:**  
Known problem student and unofficial protector of people the system misses. Teachers see incidents; some students know there is usually a reason.

**Academic tendency / subject affinity:**  
Inconsistent in formal academics. Stronger in practical, creative, physical, or hands-on tasks. Can surprise teachers when work connects to real stakes.

**Club/event ties:**  
No core club tie. Strong after-school/skate/campus-edge tie. Mirror conflict with Amelia. Light Eleanor echo through sensitivity to cruelty. Detention/discipline hook.

**Preferred locations / schedule habits:**  
Outdoor campus edges, park, hallway corners, convenience store, off-campus paths, places where rules thin out after school.

**Preferences / favorite hooks:**  
Social Preference: spontaneous plans. Free Time Preference: town exploration. Music Preference: rock/punk. Media Preference: comedy. Food Preference: spicy snacks.

Flavor hooks: skate videos, cheap energy drinks, practical repairs, stickers/graphics, low-structure hangouts.

**Best repeatable interaction types:**  
Talk outside formal spaces, park hangout, convenience-store errand, practical help, check-in after trouble, casual text, event invite that does not feel too polished.

**Possible romanceability and authored orientation:**  
Accepted: bisexual, romanceable for compatible protagonists in the full game. MVP can show warmth or protective attention, but romance is not needed.

**What the player first notices:**  
Hoodie, board, scuffed shoes, late arrival, quick grin, and a practiced talent for making authority look ridiculous.

**What the player might misunderstand:**  
That he is lazy, unserious, or naturally defiant. In truth, much of his defiance is pre-emptive self-defense and protective anger.

**Intro beat premise:**  
**"The Phone He Broke"** - The player first hears the school version: Leo smashed another student's phone because he is reckless, violent, or looking for trouble. Soon after, the player sees a smaller live echo of the same pattern. Someone makes a cruel joke or starts to socially corner a weaker student, possibly with a phone already out. Leo cuts in with a joke sharp enough to stop the room.

The moment works because Leo is funny, fast, and fearless. It also reveals the problem: he stops humiliation by turning humiliation back on the aggressor. The target is protected, the bully backs down, and the room laughs, but Leo's method leaves a bruise of its own.

The player can laugh with him, notice the protected student, challenge the counter-humiliation, or ask about the phone rumor. Leo does not fully explain himself. The beat establishes his protective instinct, his reputation as a troublemaker, and the moral mess of his justice.

**Partial 3-beat arc candidate:**  
Strong candidate for a future systems/discipline arc, but not selected. He works best in MVP as a sharp mirror to Amelia.

## Intro Beat Guardrails

These intro beats happen during the first days or weeks of the first high-school year. The class is still forming. Some students may know each other from previous schools, local social circles, clubs, sport, or Social Media, but the whole class should not already have stable, fully understood reputations for everyone.

Treat the intro beats as the **birth of class reputations**, not proof that the class has known these people for years.

Key framing rules:

- Charlotte and Amelia work naturally as early-school beats: Charlotte begins taking social control, Amelia immediately becomes the visible academic benchmark.
- Oliver's "walk it off" reputation should come mainly from sport/team circles, prior school reputation, or PE-adjacent context rather than from the whole new class already knowing his history.
- Eleanor's rumor can predate high school through prior school, local Social Media, or Charlotte-adjacent networks, but new classmates should only have fragments at first.
- Leo's phone rumor should be partial and circulating, not a complete settled legend. The live intro moment should make the player suspect there is more to the story without clearing him completely.
- Each intro beat should suggest the wound without explaining the whole backstory.
- Player responses should express protagonist style, not create a single obvious good/bad choice.
- The beats should be paced across early weeks rather than delivered as a parade of trauma on one day. Suggested rhythm: Charlotte and Amelia in Week 1, Oliver in Week 1 or 2, Leo in Week 2 or 3, with Eleanor's partial arc threaded separately.

Accepted timing rhythm: Amelia appears first because academic reputation forms immediately; Charlotte follows through Class Integration Day; Eleanor's first beat appears early as rumor fragments begin to circulate; Oliver waits for the first PE/sport context; Leo lands after the school has had time to hear partial phone/fight rumors; Eleanor's later beats require relationship buildup and should land before Harvest Evening and heavier exam pressure.

## Why Eleanor Is The Best MVP Relationship Proof

Eleanor best demonstrates the MVP relationship model because her progression depends on the player showing judgment, restraint, and respect over time.

Her arc proves:

- Bond is not enough without story flags.
- Repeatable interactions matter between authored beats.
- Social Media and rumor context can create story pressure.
- Literary Club can enrich a classmate arc without hard-gating it.
- A relationship milestone can be about protecting someone's agency rather than extracting their secret.
- Beat 3 can open the door to friendship without solving the whole wound.
