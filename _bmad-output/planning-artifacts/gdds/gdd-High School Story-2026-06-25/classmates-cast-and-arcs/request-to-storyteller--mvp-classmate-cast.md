## Purpose

Prepare a proposed MVP classmate cast for `High School Story`.

The designer wants the MVP cast to be built from recognizable high-school archetypes, but written with enough specificity that they can support nostalgia, attachment, and bittersweet reflection rather than feeling like flat tropes.

## Current Canonical Scope

MVP relationship scope:

- 5 classmates total.
- 1 classmate receives a partial story arc with 3 beats.
- 4 classmates receive 1 early/introduction beat each.
- Total MVP relationship/story content: 7 beats.
- MVP can show romantic chemistry or potential, but does not need to reach full Romantic Partner status.
- Full game target is 12 core classmates with full arcs, plus optional lightweight/background students.

Relationship system:

- Underlying Bond 0-100, exact value hidden.
- Visible tiers: Stranger, Acquaintance, Friend, Close Friend, Best Friend / Romantic Partner.
- No separate Trust/Openness meter.
- Story flags carry emotional specificity.
- Relationships grow through repeatable interactions plus authored story milestones.
- Compatibility and preferences modify relationship growth speed/quality but do not replace effort.

## Tone and Audience

Target audience: adults nostalgic for high-school life.

Primary emotional targets:

1. Nostalgia.
2. Attachment.
3. Bittersweet reflection.

Tone: believable school life with serialized teen-drama clarity. Everyday routines should feel grounded, while character arcs can use emotionally legible drama and memorable social situations.

## Relevant MVP Systems and Events

Use these existing systems as hooks for cast design:

- 15-minute time blocks.
- Lessons as active gameplay.
- Energy, Stress, Mood.
- Subject grades and academic pressure.
- Homework and tests.
- Social Media profiles/current-location signals.
- Texting with cooldowns for generic messages and authored threads for story beats.
- Money/allowance, gifts, snacks, consumables.
- Clubs:
  - Science Club.
  - Drama Club.
- MVP events:
  - Class Integration Day: school-wide social event.
  - Harvest Evening: Saturday 18:00-21:00 autumn town event, Week 8/9 recommended.
  - Science Showcase Sprint: 90-minute Science Club special event.
  - Dress Rehearsal Crisis: 90-minute Drama Club special event.
  - Week 20 exam period.

## Romance and Orientation Constraints

- Not every classmate needs to be romanceable.
- Each classmate has a specific authored orientation.
- A classmate is romanceable only for a compatible protagonist.
- Romanceability is discovered through dialogue, chemistry, choices, story moments, and relationship development, not shown as an upfront UI badge.
- Incompatible/non-romanceable close relationships should still support a meaningful Best Friend path.
- Flirt is contextual/authored tone, not a generic repeatable action.
- MVP can show chemistry or first romantic signals but should not need to reach full Romantic Partner status.

## Requested Output

Please propose **5 MVP classmates** built from high-school archetypes.

For each classmate include:

- Working name.
- Core archetype.
- How the archetype is made specific/subverted.
- Short personality summary.
- Social role in class/school.
- Academic tendency or subject affinity.
- Club/event ties, especially Science Club, Drama Club, Class Integration Day, Harvest Evening.
- Preferred locations / schedule habits.
- Preferences or favorite hooks: music, food, media, free-time style, social preference.
- Best repeatable interaction types.
- Possible romanceability and authored orientation.
- What the player first notices.
- What the player might misunderstand about them.
- One intro beat premise.
- Whether they are a good candidate for the MVP partial 3-beat arc.

Then recommend:

- Which 1 classmate should receive the MVP partial 3-beat arc.
- Why that classmate is the best MVP proof of the relationship system.
- What the 3 beats should roughly demonstrate.
- How the other 4 intro beats should distribute across school life, clubs, and Harvest Evening.

## Archetype Guidance

Use recognizable high-school archetypes, for example:

- Popular/social connector.
- Overachiever.
- Theatre kid.
- Science/nerd club student.
- Athlete.
- Quiet outsider.
- Class clown.
- Rebel/troublemaker.
- Student council/helper.
- Artist/musician.

Do not make them generic. Each archetype should have a specific contradiction, vulnerability, or pressure. The best version is readable at first glance, then more complicated once the player invests time.

## Design Constraints

- The 5 classmates should give broad coverage of the game's systems, not all cluster around one playstyle.
- At least one strong Science Club tie.
- At least one strong Drama Club tie.
- At least one classmate should be useful for Harvest Evening social/memory potential.
- At least one classmate should create academic pressure or academic contrast.
- At least one classmate should support romantic chemistry potential in MVP, but full romance is not required.
- The cast should create meaningful FOMO: the player should want to know more than one of them but lack time for all.
- Avoid making the partial-arc character the only interesting one.
- Avoid overloading every character with every system.
- Keep MVP production realistic: 5 portrait sets, 6 baseline expressions each, 1-2 extra expressions for the partial-arc character if needed.

## Evaluation Criteria

The strongest cast proposal should:

- Make the 5 classmates visually and emotionally distinct.
- Give the player different reasons to seek each person out.
- Support the existing MVP event slate.
- Demonstrate relationship progression through both systemic interaction and authored beats.
- Fit the nostalgic, attachment-driven, bittersweet tone.
- Leave room for the eventual 12-core-classmate full-game cast.
