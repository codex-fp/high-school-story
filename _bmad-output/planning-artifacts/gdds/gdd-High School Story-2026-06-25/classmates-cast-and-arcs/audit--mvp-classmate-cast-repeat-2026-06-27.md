## Verdict

The cast is now substantially more balanced than the previous pass. Each MVP classmate has:

- A recognizable high-school archetype.
- A distinct core wound.

This is acceptable for a five-person MVP cast. Oliver does not need a heavy second relationship; his light Charlotte tie helps him belong to the ensemble without stealing focus from the two major pairs.

## Duplication Check

### Charlotte vs Amelia

Both are "perfect girls," but the difference is clear.

- Charlotte performs belonging.
- Amelia performs competence.
- Charlotte's world is taste, image, social rank, and family status.
- Amelia's world is deadlines, recommendations, teachers, university, and household responsibility.

**Status:** Distinct enough.

### Oliver vs Amelia

Both carry future-through-performance pressure.

- Oliver's future is embodied: pain, sport, masculinity-coded toughness, team praise.
- Amelia's future is procedural: grades, recommendations, applications, adult trust.

**Status:** Distinct enough, provided Oliver's scenes stay physical/social rather than academic-bureaucratic.

### Eleanor vs Leo

Both accept being misunderstood to protect someone or something private.

- Eleanor withdraws and refuses to explain.
- Leo intervenes and accepts punishment.
- Eleanor protects privacy by silence.
- Leo protects vulnerability by taking blame and creating noise.

**Status:** Good contrast. This is thematic resonance, not duplication.

### Charlotte vs Eleanor

Both orbit exposure.

- Charlotte fears exposure because it reveals construction and guilt.
- Eleanor fears exposure because it invites another violation.

**Status:** Strong linked pair. The risk is not duplication; the risk is gravitational dominance over MVP attention.

### System Coverage Update

The GDD club slate has changed after designer review:

- MVP clubs are now **Science Club** and **Literary Club**.
- **Drama Club** is deferred/full-game rather than required for MVP.
- **Dress Rehearsal Crisis** is replaced by **Zine Deadline Crisis**.

This resolves the previous club mismatch.

Additional GDD rule after designer review:

- Core classmate story arcs must not require membership in a specific club.
- Club membership can add variants, insider context, better preparation, lower Stress, stronger outcomes, extra dialogue, club identity texture, or additional flags.
- The essential beat must remain accessible through non-club routes when relationship/context requirements are met.
- Route variants are alternate access paths to one canonical relationship beat. The first valid variant encountered advances the beat state and suppresses duplicate variants. Club membership can still be acknowledged through follow-up dialogue, small texture, extra context, or outcome modifiers.

Current strongest anchors:

- Social hierarchy / reputation: Charlotte, Eleanor.
- Sports/body performance: Oliver.
- Academics/recommendations: Amelia.
- Discipline/skating/direct justice: Leo.
- Science Club: Amelia.
- Literary Club: Eleanor.

The main design gap is no longer club coverage. Eleanor has been selected as the MVP partial 3-beat arc classmate. The next real decision is the exact shape of her three beats.

## Club Coverage Canon

### Science Club

Primary cast tie: **Amelia Price**.

Amelia uses Science Club as recommendation and university-track infrastructure. It gives her teacher proximity, project pressure, resume value, and a place where initiative is visibly rewarded.

Special event: **Science Showcase Sprint**.

### Literary Club

Primary cast tie: **Eleanor "Nell" Graves**.

Literary Club fits Eleanor's natural domain: private writing, anonymous authorship, poems, short stories, fragments, heartbreak writing, misreading, consent, and the fear of becoming emotionally visible again.

Special event: **Zine Deadline Crisis**.

Important guardrail: Zine Deadline Crisis should pressure Eleanor's wound through consent and authorship, not simply repeat the old leak for shock value.

Access guardrail: Eleanor's 3-beat MVP partial arc must be one core arc with route variants, not a club-gated route. Literary Club members can act from inside the zine process; non-members can enter through Nell's invitation, Bond/context, public zine context, Social Media clues, text threads, library/cafeteria/hallway encounters, or guest/helper roles.

Variant resolution guardrail: if a Literary Club member triggers the non-club version of an Eleanor beat first, the club version should not later replay as a separate beat. The relationship milestone has already resolved; later club content may acknowledge the player's membership or the resolved event through texture.
