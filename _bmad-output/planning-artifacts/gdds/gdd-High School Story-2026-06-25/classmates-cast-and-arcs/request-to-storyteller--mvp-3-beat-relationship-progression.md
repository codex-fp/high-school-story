## Purpose

The MVP partial classmate arc uses **3 story beats**. These beats should not be written as three arbitrary dramatic scenes. They should consciously model the beginning of the full relationship progression structure.

The goal is for the MVP to prove that classmate arcs grow through:

- repeatable interaction;
- Bond/tier progression;
- authored story beats;
- contextual opportunities;
- emotional specificity from story flags.

## Full Arc Structure

The full classmate story arc target is **10 beats**:

| Relationship Phase | Beats | Purpose |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Stranger | 1 | Initial encounter / first impression |
| Acquaintance | 2 | Getting to know the classmate |
| Friend | 3 | Core personal problem and personality depth open up |
| Close Friend | 2 | Vulnerability, emotional confidence, and stronger arc stakes |
| Final Path: Best Friend / Romantic Partner | 2 | Post-commitment relationship content |

## MVP Interpretation

The MVP partial arc should cover the **first 3 beats** of that structure:

1. **Beat 1: Stranger Beat**  
   First meaningful contact. The player forms a first impression, sees the public mask/archetype/rumor, and gets a reason to care or be curious.

2. **Beat 2: Acquaintance Beat 1**  
   The player gets beyond the surface. The classmate becomes more specific than their archetype. This beat should create early context, a small reveal, or a contradiction.

3. **Beat 3: Acquaintance Beat 2 / Doorway Into Friendship**  
   This should feel like the end of the acquaintance phase and the beginning of real friendship. It does not need to fully resolve the character's core wound, but it should create a meaningful trust flag, show what the deeper Friend-phase arc will be about, and make the player want to continue.

## Important Design Constraint

The beats should be supported by repeatable interactions between them.

Do not write the three beats as if they alone fully advance the relationship. The GDD pacing assumption is:

- `Stranger -> Acquaintance`: roughly 2-3 meaningful interactions plus the relevant beat.
- `Acquaintance -> Friend`: roughly 4-6 meaningful interactions plus a later beat.

For MVP, Beat 3 can strongly **signal entry into friendship**, but the full Friend-phase arc belongs beyond the MVP unless we explicitly decide otherwise.

## What Beat 3 Should Do

Beat 3 should not be "the whole secret is revealed and solved."

It should instead:

- make the classmate feel reachable as a real friend;
- establish a trust/story flag;
- reveal enough emotional truth to make the player care;
- point toward the deeper personal problem;
- create a clear reason to keep investing after MVP;
- support future Friend-phase beats.

## Application to Nell / Literary Club Context

If the partial arc is Eleanor "Nell" Graves:

- Beat 1 can show rumor, distance, first real contact, or the player's first chance to not flatten her into the school story about her.
- Beat 2 can reveal writing, guardedness, contradiction, or the difference between her public goth/rumor persona and private emotional authorship.
- Beat 3 can use `Zine Deadline Crisis` or a related writing/authorship moment to create a first real trust flag.

But Beat 3 should not require Literary Club membership. Literary Club can provide an insider route, but non-members need a valid route through invitation, Social Media/text clue, public zine context, library/cafeteria encounter, or a bounded trusted-outsider task.

## Requested Storyteller Output

When drafting the 3 beats, please label each beat with:

- relationship phase represented;
- intended tier movement or emotional movement;
- required systemic interaction buildup;
- unlock/context requirements;
- story flag created;
- member/non-member access path if club context is involved;
- how the beat sets up the future Friend-phase arc.
