## Purpose

During preparation for Eleanor "Nell" Graves's MVP partial 3-beat arc, a design concern came up around the relationship between classmate story arcs and club membership.

Eleanor is now selected as the MVP partial 3-beat arc classmate, and she is also the primary cast tie for the new MVP Literary Club and its special event:

- **Literary Club**
- **Zine Deadline Crisis**

This fits her character strongly, but it creates a possible gating risk.

## Designer Concern

Classmate story arcs should not require the player protagonist to be a member of a specific club, especially early in the arc.

The player should be able to meet Eleanor, build a relationship with her, and progress through the main beats of her MVP partial arc even if they are not interested in joining Literary Club.

Club participation can support, enrich, accelerate, modify, or add texture to classmate beats, but it should not be a hard requirement for accessing a classmate's core relationship/story progression.

This is especially important for MVP because:

- relationship arcs are a core system proof;
- players should not miss the selected partial classmate arc because they chose a different club identity;
- the MVP has limited relationship content, so gating the primary partial arc behind club membership could make the relationship system feel narrower than intended;
- classmate arcs should emerge through Bond, context, locations, texting, Social Media, story flags, and authored opportunities, not only club enrollment.

## Specific Risk With Eleanor

Eleanor naturally fits Literary Club because her accepted concept centers on:

- anonymous/semi-hidden writing;
- private authorship;
- heartbreak poems and short fiction;
- misreading and reputation damage;
- fear of emotional exposure;
- trust and consent around sharing writing.

However, if her 3-beat MVP arc is too directly built around Literary Club participation, then a non-member protagonist might lose access to the strongest relationship arc in the MVP.

We need a design rule for how Eleanor's classmate arc can use Literary Club without requiring Literary Club membership.

## Possible Design Principles

### Principle A: Club Membership Is Never Required For Core Classmate Arcs

Core classmate story beats can use club locations, club events, or club context, but access must be possible through other routes:

- relationship Bond threshold;
- Social Media clue;
- text thread;
- library/cafeteria/quiet-location encounter;
- event attendance as guest/helper;
- invitation from the classmate;
- public school event access;
- optional club-member variant if the player belongs to the club.

### Principle B: Club Membership Adds Variants, Not Access

If the protagonist is a Literary Club member, Eleanor's beat can include:

- more insider context;
- additional dialogue;
- better preparation;
- lower Stress cost;
- extra relationship flag;
- a stronger role in Zine Deadline Crisis.

If the protagonist is not a member, the beat still exists, but entry happens through:

- Eleanor asking for help outside club;
- the protagonist seeing a Social Media/local signal;
- a public zine board issue;
- library/cafeteria encounter;
- being invited as a trusted outsider;
- helping with a bounded task that does not imply membership.

### Principle C: Special Club Events Can Be Public/Guest-Accessible

`Zine Deadline Crisis` could be a Literary Club event mechanically, but not exclusively accessible to club members.

Potential access modes:

- Literary Club member: participates as a club contributor.
- Non-member with Eleanor Bond/context: invited by Eleanor for a specific trust-sensitive task.
- Non-member with school/event clue: can attend/help as a volunteer, reader, layout assistant, errand runner, or emotional support.

This keeps the club meaningful while preventing relationship gating.

## Proposed Eleanor Beat Access Model

Eleanor's 3 MVP beats could be structured as:

### Beat 1: Rumor / First Real Contact

Access should not require Literary Club.

Possible access:

- hallway/library/cafeteria encounter;
- Social Media clue;
- player witnesses a joke/comment about Eleanor;
- Eleanor leaves or protects a piece of writing;
- player can choose whether to believe the rumor or withhold judgment.

### Beat 2: Trust Through Writing

Literary Club can support this, but should not be required.

Possible access:

- text thread;
- library encounter;
- anonymous writing clue;
- quiet location meeting;
- Literary Club variant if joined.

### Beat 3: Zine Deadline Crisis / Authorship Consent Test

This can use Literary Club as the event context, but should allow non-member access through relationship/contextual invitation.

Possible access:

- club member route: player is already involved in the zine.
- non-member route: Eleanor asks the protagonist for help because she trusts them or because they noticed the issue.
- public route: a zine board / school magazine deadline creates a visible problem the player can respond to.

## Questions For Game Designer

1. Should we formalize a rule that **club membership is never required for core classmate story beats**?
2. Should club membership provide variants/bonuses/context rather than gating relationship content?
3. Can special club events like `Zine Deadline Crisis` be accessible to non-members through invitation, guest helper roles, or public school context?
4. For Eleanor specifically, should her 3-beat partial arc be designed with parallel access paths: Literary Club member route and non-member route?
5. Are there any relationship beats in MVP that may require club membership, or should that be avoided entirely?

## Storyteller Recommendation

Use **Principle A + Principle B + Principle C**:

- Core classmate arcs should never require club membership.
- Club membership should add texture, variants, better preparation, or stronger outcomes.
- Club special events may be club-owned but should support guest/non-member access when needed for classmate arcs.

For Eleanor, Literary Club should be her strongest thematic environment, not a locked door.
