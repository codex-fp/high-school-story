## Purpose

During the interactive MVP classmate cast design pass, the cast moved toward five strong high-school archetypes with linked personal dramas:

- Charlotte "Lottie" Fairchild - hyper-polished Queen Bee / perfect daughter.
- Oliver "Ollie" Grant - injured golden-boy jock.
- Eleanor "Nell" Graves - goth rumor girl / wounded writer.
- Amelia Price - teacher's pet / university-track overachiever.
- Leo Ward - rebel skater / protective troublemaker.

This pass revealed a possible mismatch between the current MVP club scope and the strongest version of the classmate cast.

The current GDD MVP clubs are:

- Science Club.
- Drama Club.

Science Club still fits the emerging cast well through Amelia Price. Drama Club is now less naturally connected to the strongest classmate concepts, especially because Eleanor's strongest identity is not performance/theatre but private writing, poetry, heartbreak fiction, and trust around authorship.

## Requested Design Review

Please reconsider whether the MVP's second club should remain **Drama Club** or shift to a **Literary Club / Creative Writing Club**.

The proposal is not to increase club count. It is to consider a direct replacement:

- Current: Science Club + Drama Club.
- Proposed: Science Club + Literary Club / Creative Writing Club.

## Why This Change Is Being Proposed

### 1. Eleanor's strongest story does not naturally belong to Drama Club

Eleanor's current accepted concept:

- She once kept an anonymous or semi-hidden writing profile.
- She posted poems, short stories, fragments, and dark romantic pieces about heartbreak.
- Charlotte was the person she trusted with the secret.
- The profile was exposed, and the school flattened her private emotional art into an "obsessive / creepy / unstable" rumor.
- Eleanor's core dramatic rule is: she would rather be misunderstood than exposed again.

This makes her a natural fit for a writing-centered club, school magazine, poetry circle, zine group, or literary society.

It does not make her a natural fit for Drama Club unless we force an adaptation layer around monologues, scripts, or backstage writing. That is possible, but it feels less clean than a Literary Club and risks bending the character around the existing club list rather than letting the best cast shape the MVP.

### 2. A Literary Club better supports the nostalgia / attachment / bittersweet tone

The game targets adults nostalgic for high-school life. A Literary Club can support:

- private writing becoming public at the wrong time;
- notebooks, poems, zines, school magazines, anonymous submissions;
- library/cafeteria/quiet-room scenes;
- intimate text-forward relationship beats;
- memory artifacts that fit the reflective tone of the project.

This may serve the relationship system more directly than a theatre-focused club for the current MVP cast.

### 3. Science Club remains covered

Amelia Price can become the strong Science Club tie without distorting her concept.

Her accepted concept:

- teacher's pet / top student;
- parentification plus invisible neglect;
- university-track obsession;
- a limited Recommendation Slot as a key pressure point.

Science Club can work as:

- resume/recommendation infrastructure;
- a place where initiative and teacher trust matter;
- an academic pressure environment;
- a natural hook for Science Showcase Sprint.

## Proposed MVP Club Revision

### Keep: Science Club

Recommended core cast tie: Amelia Price.

Possible special event revision:

- Current: Science Showcase Sprint.
- Keep as-is or lightly reframe around Amelia's Recommendation Slot.

Design purpose:

- academic ambition;
- teacher approval;
- project pressure;
- Logic / Discipline / Creativity / Charisma contributions;
- contrast with wellbeing costs.

### Replace: Drama Club -> Literary Club / Creative Writing Club

Recommended core cast tie: Eleanor "Nell" Graves.

Possible special event replacement:

- Current: Dress Rehearsal Crisis.
- Proposed: Literary Showcase Crisis / Zine Deadline / Reading Night Crisis / School Magazine Deadline.

Potential event premise:

A 90-minute club event where the Literary Club must finalize a small reading, zine issue, school magazine page, anonymous submission board, or showcase table. A private piece risks being misattributed, exposed, censored, or pulled at the last minute. The player can help through editing, layout, emotional support, practical errands, public speaking, or protecting consent around authorship.

Design purpose:

- writing as vulnerability;
- authorship and consent;
- Social Media / reputation consequences;
- Mood/Stress effects;
- relationship trust;
- text-forward production scope.

## Alternative Options

### Option A: Replace Drama Club With Literary Club

Recommended if the cast-first approach is prioritized.

Pros:

- Strongest fit for Eleanor.
- Better supports text-forward relationship beats.
- Cleanly aligns with the current MVP cast.
- May be more scope-friendly than staging/performance scenes.

Cons:

- Removes a classic high-school media archetype/event from MVP.
- Requires updating the GDD club/event slate.
- Dress Rehearsal Crisis would need replacement.

### Option B: Keep Drama Club, Add Literary Club Later

Recommended if Drama Club is considered a must-have MVP fantasy.

Pros:

- Preserves existing GDD scope.
- Literary Club can become a full-game club for Eleanor later.

Cons:

- MVP cast has no strong Drama Club tie.
- Drama Club may need a non-core NPC to carry its event.
- Eleanor's strongest system home is deferred.

### Option C: Reframe Drama Club As "Arts & Letters Club"

Hybrid compromise.

Pros:

- Keeps performance-adjacent content while allowing writing, zines, scripts, readings, posters, and literary material.
- Could support both a rehearsal-style crisis and Eleanor's authorship themes.

Cons:

- Less archetypally clean.
- May feel like a scope workaround rather than a strong fantasy.

## Storyteller Recommendation

Prefer **Option A: replace Drama Club with Literary Club / Creative Writing Club** for MVP.

Reason:

The current MVP classmate cast is strongest when clubs support the actual emotional centers of the characters. Amelia naturally carries Science Club. Eleanor naturally carries Literary Club. Forcing Eleanor into Drama Club weakens her concept, while leaving Drama Club with no core classmate tie weakens the MVP relationship/event integration.

If the MVP goal is to prove relationship progression through authored emotional beats, a writing-centered club may be more valuable than a theatre-centered club for this cast.

## Design Questions For The Game Designer

1. Is Drama Club a fixed MVP pillar, or can the second MVP club change if cast design supports a better fit?
2. Would Literary Club / Creative Writing Club satisfy the same production and gameplay role as Drama Club?
3. Should the MVP event slate change from Dress Rehearsal Crisis to a writing/publishing/reading crisis?
4. If Drama Club remains, should it be carried by non-core NPCs while Eleanor's literary content is deferred to the full game?
5. Does replacing Drama Club with Literary Club improve or weaken the intended high-school media fantasy?
