## Decision

Accepted: **core classmate story arcs must not require membership in a specific club**.

Club context can support a classmate arc, but it cannot be the only access route for the classmate's core relationship/story progression.

## Rule

For core classmate story beats:

- Club membership is not a hard requirement.
- Club membership can add variants, insider context, better preparation, lower Stress, stronger outcomes, extra dialogue, club identity texture, or additional flags.
- The essential beat must remain accessible through non-club routes when the player has the required relationship/context.

Valid non-club access routes include:

- Bond/tier threshold.
- Prior story flags.
- Social Media clue.
- Text thread.
- Library/cafeteria/hallway encounter.
- Classmate invitation.
- Public school context.
- Guest/helper role.
- Bounded task requested by the classmate.

## Club Special Events

Club-owned special events can support non-member access when required for a classmate arc.

Use parallel framing:

- **Club member route:** the protagonist participates as an insider/contributor.
- **Non-member route:** the protagonist enters as invited support, guest helper, trusted outsider, volunteer, reader, errand runner, or emotional support.

The member route can be richer or more efficient, but the non-member route must still carry the essential relationship beat.

## Eleanor / Nell Application

For Eleanor "Nell" Graves:

- Literary Club is her strongest thematic environment.
- Literary Club membership should not be required for her MVP 3-beat partial arc.
- Her arc should support parallel access paths.

Recommended structure:

### Beat 1: Rumor / First Real Contact

No club membership required.

Access can happen through hallway, library, cafeteria, Social Media clue, or witnessing a joke/comment about Nell.

### Beat 2: Trust Through Writing

Literary Club can provide an insider variant, but non-members can access the beat through a text thread, library encounter, anonymous writing clue, quiet-location meeting, or Nell choosing to share a limited piece of context.

### Beat 3: Zine Deadline Crisis / Authorship Consent Test

`Zine Deadline Crisis` can remain a Literary Club special event, but non-member protagonists can enter if relationship/context justifies it.

Routes:

- Literary Club member: already involved in the zine and can act from inside the process.
- Non-member with Nell Bond/context: Nell asks for help with a trust-sensitive task.
- Public/context route: the zine board, school magazine deadline, or visible authorship problem creates an opportunity to intervene.

## Scope Guardrail

Do not build a separate membership-gated version of Nell's arc. Build one core arc with route variants.

The player who joins Literary Club should feel better positioned and see more texture. The player who does not join Literary Club should still be able to know Nell, support her, and experience the core MVP arc.
