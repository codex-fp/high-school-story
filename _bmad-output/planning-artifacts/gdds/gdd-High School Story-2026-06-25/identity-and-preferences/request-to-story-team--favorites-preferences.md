## Current Character Creation Decisions

### Starting Attributes

The game has 6 starting attributes:

- Logic
- Creativity
- Charisma
- Discipline
- Physicality
- Empathy

Attributes:

- Use point buy.
- Use a 1-5 starting scale.
- Start at baseline 2 each.
- Player receives 6 additional points.
- Player may lower any number of attributes from 2 to 1 for extra points.
- Maximum starting attribute is 5.
- Attributes do not increase during play.
- Attributes are broad character traits, not direct subject stats.

### Identity Principle

Character creation gives the protagonist a starting shape, but the richer school identity develops during play through:

- Activities chosen.
- Relationships developed.
- Opportunities sacrificed.
- Academic investment.
- Clubs.
- Wellbeing habits.
- Story flags.
- Identity markers.

Favorites/preferences should support this principle. They should make the protagonist feel personal at the start, but not define the whole run.

---

## What Favorites / Preferences Need To Do

Favorites/preferences should:

- Make the created student feel like a specific person.
- Support relationship compatibility and classmate chemistry.
- Influence activity flavor and sometimes activity effectiveness.
- Influence Mood outcomes from activities.
- Create small roleplay hooks.
- Be useful for dialogue, texting, social media, gifts, hangouts, dates, clubs, and story beats.
- Avoid becoming a giant tag spreadsheet.

Favorites/preferences should not:

- Hard-lock major content.
- Replace relationship effort.
- Make one build obviously optimal.
- Require massive bespoke writing for every possible preference.
- Become a second attribute system.

## Design Questions For You

Please propose:

1. A recommended set of favorite/preference categories for character creation.
2. For each category, 5-10 example options.
3. What each category affects mechanically or narratively.
4. Which categories should matter strongly vs lightly.
5. Which categories should be used for compatibility with classmates.
6. Which categories should be used mostly for flavor/Mood/activity texture.
7. Any categories that sound appealing but should be avoided due to scope or ambiguity.

Please also consider whether we need:

- Favorite music style.
- Favorite movie/TV genre.
- Favorite book/literature genre.
- Favorite food/snack/drink.
- Favorite hobby/activity vibe.
- Social preference, such as quiet hangouts vs group events.
- Weekend preference, such as active / cozy / social / productive.
- Fashion/style preference.
- Any other school-life specific preference.

## Tone and Scope Guidance

The game's tone is warm, nostalgic, grounded, and lightly teen-drama flavored. Favorites should feel like natural student identity markers, not RPG build tags.

Prefer a small number of expressive categories over a large taxonomy.
