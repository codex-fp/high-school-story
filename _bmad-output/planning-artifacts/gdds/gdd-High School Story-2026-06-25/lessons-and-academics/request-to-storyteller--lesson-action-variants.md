## Current Lesson Model

- Lessons are active gameplay, not passive schedule blocks.
- Each lesson is 45 minutes.
- Each lesson contains 3 blocks of 15 minutes.
- In each block, the player chooses 1 lesson action.
- Lessons use the same core grammar as the rest of the game: spend time by choosing actions.
- Lessons are more restrictive than free time and use lesson-only actions.
- The intended feel is lightly similar to JRPG combat: the "opponents" are teacher attention and remaining lesson time.
- The player is not literally fighting the teacher.

## Risk Model

- Each teacher/lesson has a strictness level.
- Each lesson action has a risk profile.
- Risk is shown qualitatively, not as exact percentages.
- Example labels: Safe, Low Risk, Risky, Very Risky.
- Being caught usually causes a small immediate penalty.
- Larger consequences are reserved for repeated behavior, severe actions, or strict teachers.
- Lateness/truancy are not normal systems.

## Current Lesson Action Examples

- Focus / listen actively: current-subject knowledge gain, Energy cost, may increase Stress if already strained.
- Take notes: moderate current-subject gain, lower Energy cost, safe.
- Study another subject: improves another subject or upcoming deadline preparation, lightly risky.
- Use phone / text: social, message, or social media gain, risk, low or no current-subject gain.
- Quiet recovery / zone out: small Energy recovery or Stress/Mood help, low current-subject gain.
- Chat / pass note: relationship/social gain, risk.
- Risky distraction: bigger Mood/social payoff, higher risk.

## Wellbeing Model

- Energy and Stress are underlying 0-100 values.
- Energy gates activities and affects performance.
- Stress worsens outcomes, raises costs, weakens academic performance, and can feed negative Mood pressures.
- Mood is qualitative, not a one-dimensional meter.
- Mood states can include Happy, Content, Tired, Sad, Lonely, Anxious, Irritated, Depressed, Excited, Calm, Overwhelmed.
- Mood effects are mostly soft.

## What We Need From You

Please propose additional lesson actions that fit this game.

For each proposed action, include:

- **Action name**
- **Player fantasy / narrative flavor**
- **Mechanical effect**
- **Energy impact**
- **Stress impact**
- **Mood impact or emotional pressure**
- **Academic impact**
- **Relationship/social impact if any**
- **Risk profile:** Safe / Low Risk / Risky / Very Risky
- **Caught consequence suggestion**
- **When it should appear:** all lessons, specific subjects, specific teachers, specific Mood states, near exams, during classmate arcs, etc.

## Design Constraints

- Do not turn lessons into unrelated minigames.
- Do not require complex new UI for each action.
- Do not create combat, magic, supernatural, or slapstick school chaos.
- Keep actions grounded in a school-life sim with nostalgic teen-drama flavor.
- Actions should support at least one pillar:
  - Time and wellbeing as core currencies.
  - Relationships through interaction and story milestones.
  - Starting identity enriched through tradeoffs.
  - School structure as pressure.
- Prefer actions that create tradeoffs between academics, wellbeing, social opportunity, and risk.
- Avoid overproducing content-heavy one-off actions unless they are clearly authored-event actions.
