## Required Brief Corrections

### 1. Relationship Progression Model

Update the brief wherever it currently implies that relationships grow mainly through presence, repeated attention, compatibility, or engagement alone.

New intended model:

- Relationships grow through smaller interactions such as conversation, texting, meetings/hangouts, gifts, and similar contact points.
- These interactions raise relationship level between major story milestones.
- Each important classmate has a personal story arc.
- Story arcs progress through larger scripted quests/milestones rich in dialogue and events.
- Major story-arc milestones require the relationship to reach the appropriate level first.
- Compatibility is not the relationship engine by itself. It is a modifier that makes relationship growth faster or more efficient than it would be without compatibility.

Recommended brief phrasing:

> Relationships progress through a combination of repeatable interactions and authored story milestones. The player deepens bonds through conversations, texting, meetings, gifts, and other smaller contact points; after reaching the required relationship level, larger scripted story-arc quests unlock and advance that classmate's personal arc. Compatibility accelerates relationship growth but does not replace attention, effort, or interaction.

### 2. Starting Identity vs Lived Identity

Update the brief wherever it implies that the protagonist's identity is only emergent and not partly defined at character creation.

New intended model:

- Character creation defines starting identity inputs.
- Starting inputs include stats and favorites/preferences.
- Favorites/preferences can include music, films, literature, food, and similar taste markers.
- Starting stats influence aptitude and possibly compatibility.
- Favorites/preferences influence relationship texture, compatibility, activity flavor, and how the protagonist initially feels personalized.
- The richer school identity still develops during play through repeated activities, relationships, lifestyle patterns, and sacrifices.

Recommended brief phrasing:

> The protagonist begins with a light starting identity defined through stats and favorites/preferences such as music, films, literature, and food. These choices shape aptitude, compatibility, and personal texture. The fuller school identity emerges during play from repeated activities, relationships, lifestyle patterns, and the opportunities the player gives up.
