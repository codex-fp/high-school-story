## Accepted Setup Decisions

- UX workflow mode: Coaching path, with step-by-step visibility and explicit acceptance or rejection of assumptions before they enter the spines.
- Product scope: MVP-first, full-game-aware.
- MVP platform posture: PC / Steam first.
- Input posture: keyboard + mouse plus controller.
- Handheld posture: Steam Deck-conscious, with legibility and control considerations captured where relevant.
- Visual exploration posture: wireframes and mockups generated by Codex as images/SVG artifacts during discovery.
- Smartphone posture: significant UX hub, especially for planning, social discovery, school information, map/travel reference, and settings.
- Planning split: Calendar is the primary time-planning surface; Map handles location, travel, and route feasibility.
- Relationship presentation: primarily character profiles, supported by chat and social feed.
- School simulation posture: grades and exams are core MVP systems, not seasonal-only events.
- Input priority: controller-first, with keyboard as controller substitute and mouse as auxiliary input.
- Memory Ledger posture: semester/end-of-term flow, not a standalone phone app by default.
- MVP School App / Journal depth: shows grades as progress indicators for school subjects.
- Manual save placement: visible in the phone.
- Social IA correction: Classmate Profile and Relationship Progress are phone-accessible subviews inside Social / Profiles, not standalone top-level surfaces.
- Event IA correction: school events, town events, and club special events are calendar/location-driven flows that reuse calendar, map/location, activity choice, dialogue, and summary surfaces; they are not standalone top-level "modes" by default.
- Map IA correction: Travel Planning primarily belongs inside Phone Map. Top-down exploration keeps local transition/interact prompts, while route feasibility, destination review, and travel planning are phone-map responsibilities.
- Travel invocation split: travel can be initiated either from Phone Map for known destinations / fast-travel-style planning, or through world interactions such as bus stops, map edges, doors, and location exits.
- Prologue IA correction: Prologue / Arrival is a scripted narrative onboarding flow that reuses exploration, dialogue, transition/cutscene framing, activity prompts, and sleep/reflection surfaces; it is not a standalone screen.
- Calendar IA correction: Calendar Planning and Phone Calendar are the same player-facing calendar experience. Treat full planning and compact phone views as states/layouts of Phone Calendar rather than separate top-level surfaces.
- Phone naming correction: Phone Shell should be named Phone Home Screen / App Grid. Social feed, profiles, and relationship details should be named as subviews of Phone Social.
- Lesson IA clarification: lesson time-block visibility and lesson choice UI should be described separately but as parts of one Lesson Flow. Break and Lunch are school-restricted activity choice variants, not wholly separate experiences.
- Exam IA clarification: Exam Period is a school calendar/lesson-adjacent flow using shared school surfaces, but exams resolve as a quick exam execution instead of the 3x15 lesson action-block sequence. Exam Results are accessed through the school notice board and/or Phone School App.

## Phone Social Wireframe Decisions

- Phone Social navigation uses bottom tabs.
- Relationship tier/progress is primarily descriptive rather than a visible numeric/progress meter.
- Profiles do not expose a direct "next known opportunity" command by default; clues and posts route to Calendar or Map when actionable.
- Feed should lean more Instagram-like than school-bulletin-like, while still supporting school/town/club posts.
- Phone top bar is phone-system UI, not app UI: black bar, date on the left, current time centered, energy represented as a battery-style icon on the right.
- Back / Home belongs inside the app chrome, not the phone-system top bar. Use an app-level back arrow in the upper-left of the phone screen.
- Phone Social should not have an Alerts tab. Use red circular notification badges with white unread counts on relevant app buttons / tabs instead.
- Messages IA decision: Phone Messages is a standalone phone app. Phone Social does not include a Messages bottom-nav tab; profiles can expose a contextual Message shortcut that opens the standalone Messages app directly to that person's thread.

- Interaction prompts are anchored near the relevant character, object, door, bus stop, or map-edge affordance in the world rather than fixed at the bottom of the screen.

## Phone Calendar / Map Decisions

- Phone Calendar is a reference view for known commitments and automatically added events; it is not a formal planning tool where the player selects free slots or assigns activities to calendar blocks.
- Phone Calendar may lean toward a 15-minute block grid, but its purpose is to show existing lessons, lunch, clubs, exams, events, deadlines, and known timed clues.
- Phone Map should be a symbolic pixel-art town/campus map rather than a destination list with a small diagram.
- Map feasibility should communicate travel time to destination and the amount of usable time remaining at that destination before the player must travel back to the dorm or next hard commitment.
- Travel from Phone Map is always available for known locations.

## Lesson Flow Decisions

- GDD-confirmed direction: lessons are active gameplay segments with a feel lightly similar to JRPG turn combat, where the pressure sources are teacher attention and remaining lesson time. The player is not literally fighting the teacher.
- Lesson UX should use custom school-life gameplay language, not combat-copy language. Avoid HP/enemy/attack framing.
- Each lesson is 45 minutes split into three 15-minute blocks. The player chooses one lesson action per block.
- Lesson actions should be presented as stable top-level categories with contextual variant labels/tooltips, not as a huge list of all possible variants.
- Risk should be qualitative: Safe, Low Risk, Risky, Very Risky. Do not show exact percentages by default.
- Teacher/lesson strictness and action risk combine to create teacher attention/caught consequences.
- Teacher questions/checks are semi-predictable pressure events, not separate minigames.
- Lesson scene presentation should not use the standard top-down exploration camera. Prefer a classroom scene from the student's seated perspective in a third-person composition: the player protagonist is visible from behind, facing the teacher and board.
- After each 15-minute lesson block, the game should provide short feedback and may trigger a teacher check / teacher attention reaction. Feedback and checks are per-block resolution beats, not one-off moments tied only to specific blocks.
- Lesson time blocks should be visualized as a clock face / circular time dial rather than only a horizontal segmented bar.
- Teacher attention and strictness should appear inside a teacher panel that includes the teacher's name, making the pressure source feel like a specific person rather than an abstract system.

## Activity Choice + Time Cost Mockup Decisions

- Activity Choice + Time Cost is an in-world decision overlay that appears after the player interacts with a relevant NPC/object/activity occasion in the current location. It is not launched from Calendar or Map.
- First raster scenario: cafe/barista activity occasion.
- Normal exploration HUD remains visible while the activity choice overlay is open.
- Blocked activities remain visible with a plain-language reason instead of disappearing.
- The overlay must show known time cost, expected end time, money cost where relevant, wellbeing/social/academic effect hints, and feasibility against next hard commitment or dorm return.
- Activity Choice + Time Cost raster mockup v1 generated and saved to `.working/activity-choice-time-cost-raster-mockup-v1.png`.
- Camera correction accepted after Game Designer review: default Activity Choice + Time Cost uses an overlay over the same top-down exploration view, not a lesson-like or cinematic close camera.
- Close/cinematic activity presentation is reserved for heavier authored moments such as relationship milestones, club specials, exams, major events, lessons, or memory/semester reflection beats.
- Activity Choice + Time Cost raster mockup v1 should be treated as too cinematic for the default routine activity-choice pattern.
- Activity Choice + Time Cost raster mockup v2 generated and saved to `.working/activity-choice-time-cost-raster-mockup-v2.png`; v2 is the active reference for the default routine activity-choice pattern.
