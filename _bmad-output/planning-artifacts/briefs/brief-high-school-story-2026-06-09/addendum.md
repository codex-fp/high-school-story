# Addendum: High School Story

## Extracted Assumptions

1. Input is desktop keyboard and mouse because the current documented target platform is Desktop JVM.
2. Exact formulas for lesson risk, condition thresholds, academic failure thresholds, study efficiency, recovery efficiency, money pressure, and exam weighting remain open pending balancing work.
3. No licensed music or voiced dialogue is required for the MVP.

## Full-Game Vision Promoted From Brainstorming

- High School Story should be framed as a full three-year high school journey, not a one-semester game.
- The dominant promise is that the player builds their own version of high school through layered daily, weekly, semester, and yearly choices.
- Meaningful incompleteness is a product value: a full run should leave memorable paths not taken.
- Each school year should have a distinct dramatic role: entry and exploration, deepening and consolidation, culmination and future choice.
- In the complete product, every major classmate should eventually have a full story arc.
- Seasonal school events belong to the full product promise, not only to a distant roadmap note.
- The end of the full game should carry coming-of-age weight and point toward life after high school.

## Downstream GDD / Production Inputs Already Known

- MVP duration is one playable twelve-week semester.
- Weekday rhythm is fixed: dormitory morning, school block, afternoon freedom, dormitory evening, sleep.
- Weekends should feel structurally different and support stronger recovery and longer social opportunities.
- Academic collapse is the only hard fail floor.
- Relationship rewards should remain primarily emotional and content-based rather than systemic.
- Known classmates expose discovered preferences, current location, and availability through an in-world social layer.

## Still-Unresolved Design Questions

- How many classmates and full arcs define the complete launch version of the game?
- Which school events and calendar milestones are essential to the three-year promise?
- How should Year 3 and graduation resolve future-facing choices?
- What exact formulas govern risk, recovery, and academic danger?
- What are the names, roles, and route owner for the five MVP classmates?
- Which profile categories are public, discoverable, private, or route-specific?
- How many starting interest toggles and academic point-buy points should setup expose?
- What should the post-MVP expansion path look like after the first semester succeeds?

## Purpose Of This Addendum

This addendum keeps unresolved but already-known downstream design inputs visible without bloating the core brief. It should feed later `gds-gdd`, balancing, cast-definition, post-MVP scope planning, and PRD work.
