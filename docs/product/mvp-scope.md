# MVP Scope

## Overview

The MVP of High School Story is intended to validate the core fantasy and systemic foundation of the game rather than deliver the full three-year experience. The goal is to prove that the player can live through a meaningful slice of school life driven by time, movement, interaction, and social context.

## MVP Goal

The MVP should demonstrate that High School Story already feels like the intended game, even in a reduced scope. It should prove that the core mechanics work together inside a playable school-life loop.

Success statement:

If the MVP includes several working story arcs and all core mechanics are in place, it should already feel recognizably like High School Story.

## In Scope

The MVP must include:

- movement
- interaction
- travel
- dialogues
- day-night cycle
- one playable semester
- several working story arcs in lightweight form
- a small class roster of 5 classmates
- 3 core locations

## MVP Locations

The first playable version must include:

- dormitory
- school
- the outdoor district containing both locations

## MVP Content Scale

- Time span: 1 semester
- Class size in MVP: 5 classmates
- Story content: several story arcs, but not fully expanded versions
- Structure: enough content to exercise the daily loop, lessons, travel, interaction, and social progression

## Core Systems Required

- The player can move through the world.
- The player can interact with locations, objects, and characters.
- The player can travel between relevant places.
- The game tracks time across the day.
- The game enforces the school-day structure and dormitory return rules.
- The game supports dialogue interactions.
- The game supports at least a basic relationship and story progression framework.

## Out of Scope

The MVP does not need:

- fully expanded story arcs for the full cast
- full three-year progression
- full-semester variety of locations beyond the defined MVP set
- complete content scale for the final class size
- polished breadth of events expected from the final game

## Why This Scope

- It is the smallest slice that still tests the identity of the game.
- It validates whether time, place, and interaction already create meaningful daily play.
- It allows narrative and relationship systems to be tested without requiring final content scale.
- It reduces production risk before building the full multi-year structure.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The player can complete a playable loop covering daily life within one semester.
- The player can move between the dormitory, school, and district.
- The player can trigger interactions and dialogues in the world.
- The player can experience time passing as part of normal decision-making.
- The player can engage with at least several lightweight story arcs.
- The MVP contains 5 classmates that can participate in the social loop.
- The combined experience is sufficient for the creator to judge that the game's core identity is present.
